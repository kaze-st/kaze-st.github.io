WeFund

WeFund is a project that dedicates to community building and to provide people in need with donations and bartering. Our main goal for this project is to ensure that for the people who have unwanted items, they are able to barter or donate it away, so will not go to waste. Our main functionality of the project is a donation pool. In this donation pool, group members will be able select their own wish item and donate to the pool. Once the pool reaches the necessary funds for the item with the most priority, the user who wished that item will be able to purchase it. There are two factors that determines which item would go first in this priority queue. First, the most intuitive factor, the longer the item remains in the queue, the more priority points it will get. Lastly, using priority votes, users can upvote an item’s priority in case the it has to be purchased urgently. However, we do ensure that people do not take advantage of the system by allowing users to block the item with the most priority the money in the pool is frozen. With these features, our team wants to give the opportunity to the people who can give to donate for the cause and the people who are in need to claim for the necessary help. Finally, we included a group chat and a personal chat system that allows the members of a specific group to be able to communicate within them in order to discuss about the donation pool.

DeafHacks

In my computer science lectures, I always encounter an interpreter who uses American Sign Language to translate the professor’s lecture to a deaf student. Recently, I have seen many technologies that helps the disabled such as Talkitt for the deaf and Be My Eyes for the blind. The promising intersection between technology and disability is never far my mind. With this inspiration in mind, I wanted to create a similar app that could help my people like my deaf classmate for DefHacks. The organizers for this hackathon provided us with Microsoft latest Hololens, which for me it was the perfect technological tool for people who cannot hear since they can use their sight. I came out with the idea to use the speech to text algorithm provided by Hololens to display the captions of the speaker on the holograms, resembling the subtitles of a movie. All the user has to do is to “click” a holographic button and that will start the program to display the captions We are very proud of our final product, but we were still surprised and humbled when it won Best Hack for Accessibility.

Source code: https://github.com/hpat1996/Hololens_SpeechToText
